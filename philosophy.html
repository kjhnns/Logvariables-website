Life is Inputs and Outputs. Connect the Dots.

Your well-being isn't magic; it's cause and effect. Variables helps you distinguish between what you control (Inputs) and how you feel (Outputs).

1

Track the Inputs (The Levers)

These are the independent variables you control.

  • Caffeine intake
  • Phone location
  • Social hours
2

Observe the Outputs (The Results)

These are the dependent variables you want to optimize.

  • Morning energy
  • Focus quality
  • Anxiety levels
3

Find the Pattern

Use our SQL-powered engine to ask: "Does working from the couch (Input) actually lower my anxiety (Output), or just my productivity?"

Log the Story, Not the Spreadsheet.

You don't need a stopwatch. You just need to recount the "story" of your day. Variables is built for speed using Voice and Shortcuts.

Just press a button and say:

"Social battery low, I had 3 drinks, sleep was restless, and I read 20 pages."

We parse it into data:

Social_Battery: Low (Enum)
Alcohol_Count: 3 (Numeric)
Sleep_Quality: Restless (Enum)
Pages_Read: 20 (Numeric)

Experiment A: The "Brain Fog" Detective

The Question: Is my afternoon crash caused by lunch carbs or poor sleep?
Track Inputs: Lunch_Type (Carbs vs. Salad), Sleep_Hours.

Experiment B: The "Introvert's" Balance

The Question: How much socializing can I handle before I burn out?

The Insight: You realize 3 hours with friends is fine, but 1 hour of networking tanks your mood for 24 hours.
